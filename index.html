Minerals

A picture and a brief set of information of various well known rocks and minerals.

Gold

Gold in its purest from is a bright yellow. It is admired for its beauty and is a commonly used as decoration for jewellery. It is softer than most minerals and can bend and break easily with simple tools.

Back To The Top

Iron

Iron is a common mineral that is usually refined to produce steel. Pure iron is usually soft, but if it is refined then it is usually hardened and tougher.

Back To The Top

Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and is mainly used in construction because of it is very tough and cheap. It also used in some other every day materials, such as scissors.

Back To The Top

Copper

Copper is a bright reddish-brown mineral. It used be made to make weapons and coins, but now it is commonly used as wiring tools.

Back To The Top

Diamond

A very rare mineral. It is usually used for jewellery. In it's pure form, it is black. But once polished and refined it becomes crystal clear.

Back To The Top

Jade

The jade is a hard, green mineral that is commonly used in jewellery. In the Chinese empire, jade signified great importance and power.

Back To The Top

Silver

Silver is like a more common and cheaper version of gold. It is used in jewellery and some forms of cutlery.
